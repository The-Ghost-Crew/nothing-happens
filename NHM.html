alchemist's journal — entry i

I don't understand. I was a simple alchemist — well, not really a simple one. I made many things and experienced a lot of ideas, but most of my work ended in nothing. Nothing happening, over and over. It drives me mad. I accumulated thousands of elements, thousands of possibilities, but my mind wanted more. Nothing happens, and it happens again and again. But then something happened. I didn't know it then, but now I understand — I shouldn't have messed with a concept I didn't know. I have to get out, or at least survive. If I survive, I can return. I won't make the same error.

I can't understand it. It does seem in vain, but no cruel person would make an almost insanely large maze just to torment someone who's done wrong. I feel so exhausted. Death, and death, and I keep going. But why. I have to get out. There is a way, but nothing. It watches me painfully. It gets so hard to see justice in this cruel punishment — it isn't jestful. It all fades into black-and-white. There is an exit somewhere, I know it. But the walls change. Always.

My eyes betray me most of all. Everything that was color is gone — I see only shades of grey now, as if the world drained alongside me. Even the halls feel wrong, too straight, too square, corners where there should be none, like something reshaped this place into blocks and lines just to mock the mind that built it. And still my body does not tire. I should have collapsed by now — days ago, weeks maybe — but my legs keep moving, my breath keeps coming, endless, tireless, and somehow that is worse than exhaustion ever was. To have no excuse to stop. To only be able to keep going. Forever, it feels. Unbearable, and still I persist.

(the ink smears past this point — the rest is unreadable, for now.)
